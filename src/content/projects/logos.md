## Data Provenance: Trust Through Transparency

In a world drowning in information, **knowing where data comes from matters as much as the data itself.**

Logos implements the [W3C PROV-DM standard](https://www.w3.org/TR/prov-dm/) ‚Äî a formal model for describing the provenance of digital artifacts. Every piece of information in Logos carries its complete lineage:

### Why Provenance Matters

- **Combating Misinformation** ‚Äî When tracking false narratives, you need to prove the chain of evidence. Who said what, when, and where did they get it?
- **Research Integrity** ‚Äî Academic and policy research requires citations. Logos maintains source attribution at every step of extraction and transformation.
- **Reproducibility** ‚Äî Any claim in Logos can be traced back to its original source, through every processing step.
- **Audit Trails** ‚Äî When information influences decisions, stakeholders need to verify the path from raw data to insight.

### The PROV Model

Every document, entity, and insight in Logos is connected through three core concepts:

- **Entities** ‚Äî Things that exist: documents, extracted facts, computed summaries
- **Activities** ‚Äî Things that happen: ingestion, extraction, classification, linking
- **Agents** ‚Äî Who or what is responsible: sources, algorithms, human reviewers

This isn't just metadata ‚Äî it's a complete graph of responsibility and derivation. When Logos says "Organization X made Claim Y on Date Z," you can trace that assertion back through the extraction activity, to the source document, to the original publication.
