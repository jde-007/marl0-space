## Market Overview

**Primary Destination:** Aspen, Colorado  
**Region:** Roaring Fork Valley, Rocky Mountains  
**Elevation:** 7,908 ft  
**Best Seasons:** Winter (skiing) and Summer (hiking, festivals)

Aspen sits at the pinnacle of American ski culture—four mountains, world-class terrain, and a historic Victorian mining town that's evolved into a cultural powerhouse hosting the Aspen Ideas Festival, Food & Wine Classic, and year-round arts programming.

## Intelligence Summary

### Key Findings

- **WikiVoyage:** 18 linked destinations including Denver, Glenwood Springs, and wine country
- **Four Mountains:** Aspen Mountain, Aspen Highlands, Buttermilk, Snowmass
- **Cultural Identity:** Beyond skiing—Aspen Institute, Wheeler Opera House, museums
- **Summer Draw:** Music festivals, hiking, mountain biking, fly fishing

### Core Attractions

1. **Aspen Mountain (Ajax)** — 675 skiable acres, Silver Queen Gondola, expert terrain
2. **Snowmass** — 3,362 skiable acres, family-friendly, largest of the four
3. **Maroon Bells** — Most photographed peaks in North America
4. **Downtown Aspen** — Victorian architecture, high-end shopping, dining
5. **Aspen Music Festival** — Summer classical music institution since 1949

## Sample Itineraries

### Winter Ski Week
- **Day 1:** Arrive, gear up, explore downtown
- **Day 2-3:** Aspen Mountain and Highlands (experts)
- **Day 4:** Snowmass (family/intermediates)
- **Day 5:** Buttermilk or backcountry tour
- **Day 6:** Spa day, shopping, fine dining
- **Day 7:** Final runs, departure

### Summer Escape
- **Day 1:** Arrive, acclimate to altitude
- **Day 2:** Maroon Bells hike (shuttle required)
- **Day 3:** Mountain biking or fly fishing
- **Day 4:** Hot springs at Glenwood Springs
- **Day 5:** Festival event or cultural programming
- **Day 6-7:** Additional hiking, departure
